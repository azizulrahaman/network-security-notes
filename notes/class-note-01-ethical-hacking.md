## 1. Definition of Ethical Hacking

Ethical hacking is the authorized practice of identifying vulnerabilities in systems, networks, and applications to improve security posture.

Unlike malicious hacking, ethical hacking operates under:
- Legal authorization
- Defined scope
- Professional ethics

---

## 2. Goals of Ethical Hacking

- Identify security weaknesses
- Simulate real-world attacks
- Strengthen defensive controls
- Reduce organizational risk
- Support compliance requirements

---

## 3. Types of Hackers

### White Hat
Security professionals who operate with authorization.

### Black Hat
Malicious attackers exploiting vulnerabilities for personal gain.

### Gray Hat
Operate between ethical and unethical boundaries.

---

## 4. Ethical Hacking Methodology

1. Reconnaissance (Information Gathering)
2. Scanning
3. Enumeration
4. Gaining Access
5. Maintaining Access
6. Covering Tracks

This structured approach mirrors real-world penetration testing frameworks.

---

## 5. Core Security Principles

### CIA Triad
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

### Risk Components
- Asset
- Threat
- Vulnerability
- Exploit
- Impact

---

## 6. Legal & Compliance Considerations

Ethical hackers must:
- Obtain written authorization
- Define scope clearly
- Avoid exceeding permission boundaries
- Follow responsible disclosure practices

Unauthorized testing is illegal under computer crime laws.

---

## 7. Common Tools Introduced

- Nmap
- Wireshark
- Metasploit
- Burp Suite
- Password cracking tools

---

## 8. Real-World Application

Ethical hacking supports:
- SOC operations
- Incident response
- Red team exercises
- Security auditing
- Regulatory compliance

---

## Key Takeaways

Ethical hacking is a proactive defense strategy that strengthens cybersecurity posture by identifying weaknesses before adversaries exploit them.
